⚙ Bridge Setup

This app is the bridge — adb ships inside it and streams logs straight from a USB-connected device. No Python, no extra scripts.

1

On the Android device, enable Developer options (tap Build number seven times in Settings → About phone) and turn on USB debugging.

2

Plug the device into this computer with a USB cable. If your cable is power-only, swap it for a data cable.

3

On the phone, accept the Allow USB debugging? RSA fingerprint prompt. Tick Always allow from this computer if you'd rather not see it again.

4

Hit the refresh icon (↻) next to the Android device picker — your device should appear in the dropdown. Then click Connect.

This app is the bridge — idevicesyslog ships inside it and streams logs from a USB-connected iOS device. No Python, no extra scripts.

1

Plug the iOS device into this computer with a USB cable. On the phone, tap Trust This Computer when prompted, then enter the device passcode to confirm.

2

Windows only: install Apple Devices from the Microsoft Store — it ships the kernel driver and Apple Mobile Device Service that iOS USB log streaming relies on. (Install Apple Devices.) macOS and Linux already have everything they need.

3

Hit the refresh icon (↻) next to the iOS device picker — your device should appear in the dropdown. Then click Connect.
